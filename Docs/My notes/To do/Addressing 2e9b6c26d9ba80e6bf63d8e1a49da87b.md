# Addressing

Plus addressing (also known as "sub-addressing") is a built-in Gmail feature that lets you create infinite variations of your email address without changing your actual account. It is a powerful way to organize your inbox, track who is selling your data, and manage subscriptions.How it WorksTo use it, simply add a plus sign (+) and any word or number after your username but before the @gmail.com. * Primary Address: yourname@gmail.com * Plus Address: yourname+newsletters@gmail.com * Plus Address: yourname+shopping@gmail.comAny email sent to these variations will still land in your primary inbox automatically. You don’t need to "enable" anything in settings to start giving these addresses out.Best Ways to Use Plus Addressing| Use Case | Example Address | Benefit ||---|---|---|| Track Spam | name+storename@gmail.com | If you get spam sent to this specific address, you know exactly which store leaked or sold your data. || Organize Subscriptions | name+news@gmail.com | Keep all your reading material under one searchable tag. || Filter Priorities | name+urgent@gmail.com | Give this only to family/emergency contacts and set a filter to "Always mark as important." || App Testing | name+test1@gmail.com | Perfect for developers who need to create multiple accounts for the same service. |How to Automatically Filter Plus AddressesThe true power of this feature comes from setting up Filters so these emails don't just clutter your main inbox. * Open Gmail on a desktop and click the Show search options icon (the sliders) in the search bar. * In the To field, type your plus address (e.g., yourname+shopping@gmail.com). * Click Create filter. * Choose what you want Gmail to do. Popular options include:   * Apply the label: Automatically categorize them.   * **Skip the Inbox (Archive it): Keep them out of your main view until you're ready to read them.   * Mark as read: Good for receipts you want to keep but not see. * Click Create filter again to save.Important Limitations * Sending Mail: While you can receive mail at these addresses, you cannot easily send from them unless you add them as a "Send mail as" alias in your Gmail Account settings. * Website Rejection: Occasionally, poorly coded websites may not recognize the + sign as a valid character in an email field. If a site gives you an error, you may have to use your standard address.Would you like me to walk you through the steps to set up a "Send mail as" alias for one of your plus addresses?Organize your Gmail Inbox with Plus AddressingThis video provides a visual walkthrough of how to create filters and labels specifically for emails sent to your plus-addressed aliases.

Access Your Settings * Open Gmail. * Click the Settings (gear icon) in the top right, then select See all settings. * Click on the Accounts and Import tab.2. Add the Alias * Look for the section labeled "Send mail as:" and click Add another email address. * A yellow pop-up window will appear. * Name: Enter the name you want people to see (e.g., "Your Name - Newsletter"). * Email address: Enter your full plus-addressed email (e.g., yourname+news@gmail.com). * Keep "Treat as an alias" checked. This ensures replies to these emails still come to your main inbox. * Click Next Step. Because it's your own Gmail account, you won't need to go through a complex verification process.3. To turn your new plus-address into an automated system, you’ll want to create a filter that applies a label (folder) as soon as an email arrives.Here is how to set it up:1. Create your Label (Folder)Before setting the rule, create a place for these emails to go: * On the left sidebar of Gmail, scroll down and click More. * Click Create new label. * Give it a name (e.g., "Shopping," "Newsletters," or "Work Projects"). * Click Create.2. Create the Automatic Rule * In the Gmail search bar at the top, click the Show search options icon (the three sliders on the right side). * In the To field, type your specific plus address: yourname+label@gmail.com. * Click Create filter (next to the blue Search button). * Check the box for Apply the label and select the label you just created from the dropdown. * Optional but recommended:   * Check Skip the Inbox (Archive it) if you want these emails to go straight to the folder without cluttering your main view.   * Check Also apply filter to matching conversations if you want to move old emails sent to that address into the folder right now. * Click Create filter.Pro Tip: The "Global" FilterIf you use many different plus addresses and want to see them all in one place, you can use a "wildcard" search in the filter.In the Has the words field of the filter settings, type: deliveredto:yourname+*@gmail.comThis will catch any email sent to you that uses a plus sign, allowing you to give one single label (like "Aliases") to all of them.Would you like me to explain how to use the "dot trick" (e.g., y.o.u.r.n.a.m.e@gmail.com) to bypass websites that don't allow plus signs?

The "dot trick" is a unique Gmail feature based on the fact that Google completely ignores periods in your username. This is helpful because many websites that block the + symbol will still accept emails with extra dots.How it WorksGmail views the following addresses as identical: * johnsmith@gmail.com * j.ohnsmith@gmail.com * john.smith@gmail.com * j.o.h.n.s.m.i.t.h@gmail.comNo matter where you place the dot, the email will land in your main johnsmith@gmail.com inbox.Why use the Dot Trick?| Scenario | Strategy ||---|---|| Site blocks + signs | Use j.ohnsmith@gmail.com to sign up instead. || Free Trial limits | Some sites see john.smith and johnsmith as two different people, letting you reuse your email for multiple trials. || Security Tracking | Use johns.mith@company.com. If you get a "win a prize" email addressed to johns.mith, you know that specific company leaked your data. |How to Filter "Dotted" EmailsFiltering these is slightly different than plus-addressing because the "To" field might just show your standard address. * Click the Show search options (sliders) in the Gmail search bar. * In the To field, type the exact dotted version you used (e.g., j.ohnsmith@gmail.com). * Click Create filter. * Apply a label like "Dot-Alias" or "Trial Account."A Critical WarningWhile Gmail ignores dots, other services do not. * Facebook, Netflix, and Banks see john.smith@gmail and johnsmith@gmail as two different accounts. * If you sign up for a service with a dot, you must use that exact dot every time you log in, even though the emails all go to the same Gmail inbox.Would you like to know about the "googlemail.com" trick, which is a third way to create an alias for your account?
